Film Textures

 This image was created both with 3D Studio 4 and Adobe Photoshop. It is interesting in how the textures to create the rolls of film were created. Basically 3D Studio's powerful image mapping capabilities did all the work from a single image. First I selected an image or a texture whatever you prefer to call it, the green dye in the water is a good example and simply edited it in Photoshop to give it the single negative look by applying a single grey line at the bottom of the image. Once done, I finally applied simple mapping coordinates to the end of the thin 3D Studio objects and instantly I had rolls of film. 3D Studio is an incredible program!

The sphereMelter

 The sphereMelter was created using 3D Studio. What's suprising here is as kewl as the image looks it was really extremely easy to create. The image is actually a single frame of a 1000 frame animation. To create the animation I jumped to frame 1000 and rotated the fan back several dozen times, then when you play the animation the fan moves perfectly. I used the same technique to create the morphing ball. I moved to frame 1000 and pulled back portions of the sphere. Once you play the animation the sphere slowly starts to melt or blow away. 3D Studio has a wealth of tools to make these process so much easier and simpler to do. The walls are just simple blocks with a brick texture applied/mapped to them. The objects that hold the sphere were extremely easy to create using 3D Studio's lofter. All I had to do was create a basic 2D shape then loft it just a little bit.

Razor Sharp Badge

 I named this the badge because it resembles a badge of some sort. What was unique about this image is how the neat shapes and selections were created. An experienced Photoshop user will notice that on some of the objects the corners are smooth in one area and pointed sharp in others. In order to achieve these types of selections or neat shapes you have to master the techniques used to collapse the midtones and how to cut/delete and re-attach shapes. It's easy with a little practice. :) I'm still working on this one...
